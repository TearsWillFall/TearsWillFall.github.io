About Me

Researcher/Bioinformatician

I’m a second year PhD student at the University College London (UCL) Cancer Institute. I work as Bioinformatician in the Treatment Resistance group with the Dr. Gerthardt Attard in an interdisciplinary team.

We aim to identify cancer vulnerabilities to be exploited therapeutically and design clinical trials of biomarker-directed strategies for rational treatment selection and sequencing.

Prior to joining the Attards Lab, I did my master in Bioinformatics at UMU and my undergraduate in Biology in UA in Spain.

My primary research interests are in machine learning, multi-level data integration and signal processing applied to clinical and molecular data, especially cell-free DNA fragmentomes.

My work is supported by the Cancer Research UK (CRUK) through the PRIME Accelerator Award which enable progress in translational cancer research through the development of tools, platforms and skills that form the foundations of such research.
